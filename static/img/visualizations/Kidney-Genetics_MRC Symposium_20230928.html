Genetic insights increasingly influence the understanding and treatment of kidney diseases. Hundreds of genes associated with monogenic
kidney disease have been identified. However, the lack of a unified and standardized database of genes assigned with kidney diseases has
led to diagnostic blind spots and comparability issues among current studies of kidney genetics. To address this gap, we created Kidney-
Genetics, a regularly updated, automated and publicly accessible database of all relevant genes associated with kidney disease:
https://github.com/halbritter-lab/kidney-genetics

By utilizing Kidney-Genetics, clinicians, geneticists, and
researchers can examine genomic data and improve their
understanding of the genetic components of diverse kidney
diseases. A standardized pipeline and automated system keep
the database on the cutting edge of kidney research and
diagnostics.
Contact: Rank Nina | nina.rank@charite.de | https://github.com/halbritter-lab/kidney-genetics
We compiled kidney disease-associated gene information from five sources (PanelApp, Literature, Diagnostic panels, HPO, and PubTator)
and created a standardized evidence-scoring system (0 5) to distinguish highly confirmed disease genes from candidate genes. Each
passing of an evidence filter criterion contributed to 1 point each in the final evidence count.
